Projects:

The projects can be categorized into two broad types:

  • Academic

    • Thermal Conductivity and Interfacial Conductivity of Graphene and MoS2

      • Refined opto-thermal Raman (OTR) technique (improved by Zhang Group) leading to the first measurements of the thermal conductivity of 1- and 2-layer MoS2 and MoSe2 and the thermal properties of hBN encapsulated 2D devices.

    • Strain engineering of Graphene and MoS2

      • Uniaxial Strain applied on Flexible and rigid substrates (within 10%) for extent of buckling facilitates, tuning of the band gap by external application of an electric field or some in-plane mechanical strain. Opening and controlling of bandgap, co-relation with temperature with applications like strain gauge and wearable electronics

    • Heterostructures and Moire Superlattices

      • 2D atomic layer heterostructures in forms of nanoribbons as well as stacked configurations were fabricated and investigated for new emergent physical phenomena, allowing for potential new devices/circuits applications. using van der Waals transfer technique, chemical doping, along with other novel manufacturing methods, low dimensional heterostructures, flexible electronic, and origami devices were realized contributing to the fields of fluid filtration and radiation shielding.

    • 2-Laser Optothermal Raman Technique for figure of merit (ZT)

      • Extending existing OTR technique in measuring thermal properties of 2D materials, group invented a dual laser at same side (DLSS) opto-thermal Raman system and incorporated a room-temperature magnetic field for decoupling of heat generation and propagation. Additional variation inclusive: Temperature, Incident Laser Power and Strains

    • Special Mention: Publications (in peer review)

      • “Emerging Two-Dimensional Nanomaterials for Biomedical Applications: From Drug/Gene delivery to Combating COVID-19” (Peer Review) ACS -2022

  • Professional

    • Varying Porosity effect on Fatigue Life for Dental Implants

      • Optimization of porosity parameters for a new range of dental implants, using data from CT scans to simulate fatiguerelationship between porosity and pore size and mechanical behavior. Finding the best set of parameters (Porosity and pore size) for average life of each implant to be 10^8 cycles and making appropriate recommendations to the manufacturer

    • Stable and Feasible solutions for airfoil flow analysis for NACA airfoil using numerical methods and Meshing techniques

      • Using two methods: an algorithm that generates the boundary condition values and modification of the mathematical B-spline curve and hit and trial method for reducing friction drag co-efficient over the profile and optimized morphing resulted in significant augmentation. Friction co-efficient reduced by 6%; 5% increase in laminar run.
